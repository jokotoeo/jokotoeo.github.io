My Projects

1-2-3 ADL Project

Through this project, I got an opportunity to learn about healthcare as well as the world of the design and the impact design has on the end user, and with this project infection prevention was the primary focus. Studies conducted by Cincinnati Children's Hospital show that infection prevention leads to less hospitalization which means lower cost for the individual. A task management device (with customized hardware) known as the 1-2-3 Device was developed in previous years, this device was designed to help patients track their activities of daily living (bathing, exercise, and oral care) which serve to help prevent infections. With this project the team was task to redesign the 1-2-3 Device (due to reliability issues due to problems with the hardware) into something more modern and impactful. I got to do user research and develop a web application that would be used to conduct testing to determine whether or not a mobile application with incentivization is needed to increase adherence to the activities of daily living and to see how a mobile application will complete with the plethora of applications that exist on mobile devices. The team and I conducted staff, parent, and patient interviews. The insights gained from those interviews were used to then determine what aspects (hardware, software, look and feel) of the device and infections prevention system needed to be improved.
